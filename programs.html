Our Work in Action

Where passion meets purpose — discover how sports transform lives across Kwale.

Safety First

The Safety First initiative, launched on December 6, 2023, aims to address sports-related injuries in Kwale County. In partnership with Base Titanium, Kenya Red Cross, and FKF Kwale, we trained 16 athletes from 8 local teams. The program focuses on improving first aid knowledge, providing essential equipment, and creating policies to ensure a safer sports environment.

The initiative also promotes women’s sports and aims to bridge the gender funding gap. Research revealed a significant lack of first aid awareness and inadequate sports facilities and equipment. Key recommendations include better training, safer playing environments, and comprehensive health records for athletes.

TWALENGA Women in Sports

Twalenga is more than a program — it’s a movement. We champion women’s participation and leadership in sports by offering mentorships, coaching clinics, and public awareness campaigns. The initiative builds confidence among young girls and creates role models in traditionally male-dominated spaces. Through Twalenga, we’re creating a new generation of empowered women shaping the future of Kenyan sports.

Sports Nutrition

Our Sports Nutrition program educates athletes and their families on the role of diet in performance and recovery. From hosting cooking demos using local ingredients to personalized meal plans, we ensure athletes have the fuel they need to excel. The initiative also addresses food insecurity and advocates for balanced nutrition in schools and communities.

Sports Tourism

The Sports Tourism program merges sport with culture and conservation. We host tournaments along Kwale’s stunning beaches and historic sites, attracting teams from across Kenya. These events boost local tourism, create jobs, and promote cultural exchange. By combining competition with travel, we showcase Kwale’s natural beauty while stimulating economic development.

Save Our Sables

The Save Our Sables campaign is a community initiative to protect the endangered Roosevelt Sable Antelope found only in Shimba Hills, Kwale.

“The Sable is not just an animal. It is a living memory, a breath of the wild soul of Kwale.”

Sports Safety Campaign

The Sports Safety Campaign focuses on creating safer environments for young athletes across Kwale County. We provide protective gear to teams in need, train coaches in first aid and emergency response, and conduct school visits on injury prevention and wellness. This campaign has reached hundreds of youth, ensuring that sport remains a source of health and joy, not harm.
