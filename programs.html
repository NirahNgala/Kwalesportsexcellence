Save Our Sables Campaign

Sports have proven to have the ability to champion social causes around the world. Time and time again, sports have been used to rally communities, raise awareness, and fundraise by providing platforms for important issues.

The Save Our Sables campaign is a coordinated initiative to conserve the rare Roosevelt Sable Antelope (Hippotragus Niger Roosevelt), found only in the Shimba Hills National Reserve, Kwale County.

“The Sable is not just an animal. It is a living memory, a breath of the wild soul of Kwale. If it vanishes, a piece of us goes with it.”

Our Campaign Activities

Cycle for Sables

A flagship cycling event raising awareness and funds for Sable conservation. The inaugural ride took place on 16th March 2024 led by Ntsiki Bidii, rallying the community behind the conservation cause.

Exposure Tours

Game Drive

Guests embark on a thrilling game drive through the Shimba Hills National Reserve to explore its rich biodiversity, spot wildlife, and appreciate the natural habitat of the endangered Roosevelt Sable Antelope.

Bush Breakfast

After the early morning drive, participants enjoy a serene outdoor breakfast surrounded by nature — a chance to connect with the wild while supporting conservation tourism.

Sable Antelope Tracking

Guided adventures through the reserve to track, observe, and learn about the behavior and movements of the endangered Sable Antelope — creating a deeper appreciation for their conservation.

Community Education Barazas

Organizing local forums (barazas) where community members gather to discuss conservation practices, learn the cultural significance of Sables, and pledge to protect Kwale’s natural heritage.

Sports Safety Campaign

Education, gear, and injury prevention for young athletes.
